Seneca Falls Convention

The 5 Organizers

Elizabeth Cady Stanton and Lucretia Mott, two of the convention's organizers, met at the World Anti-Slavery Convention with their husbands. Stanton and Mott were excluded from attending on the basis of sex, so they agreed to hold a woman's rights convention. The 5 organizers of this convention are shown below, and you'll have the opportunity to meet them if you come.




Elizabeth Cady Stanton

Stanton is the lead organizer for the Seneca Falls Convention and a passionate woman's rights advocate. She was exposed to law from a young age because of her father, a prominent and successful attorney. She studied at Troy Female Seminary and fought for woman's property rights reform in 1840.

Lucretia Mott

Mott is a Quaker preacher from Philadelphia, and known for her anti-slavery, women’s rights and religious reform activism.

Mary M'Clintock

M'Clintock is the daughter of Quaker anti-slavery, temperance and women’s rights activists. In 1833, M’Clintock and Mott organized the Philadelphia Female Anti-Slavery Society.

Martha Coffin Wright

Mott's sister, Wright is a long-time abolitionist and woman's rights activist. She ran a station in the Underground Railroad from Auburn, New York.

Jane Hunt

Hunt is also a Quaker activist and a member of M'Clintock's extended family through marriage.
