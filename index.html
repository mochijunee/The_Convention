Seneca Falls Convention

“I would have girls regard themselves not as adjectives but as nouns.”
- Elizabeth Cady Stanton

Who: Women on the first day, open to men on the second day

What: A woman's rights convention

When: July 19 and 20, 1848

Where: Wesleyan Chapel, Seneca Falls, New York

Why: “We are assembled to protest against a form of government, existing without the consent of the governed—to declare our right to be free as man is free, to be represented in the government which we are taxed to support, to have such disgraceful laws as give man the power to chastise and imprison his wife, to take the wages which she earns, the property which she inherits, and, in case of separation, the children of her love.” - Elizabeth Cady Stanton

Visit the "Guests" and "Events" page to learn more about the convention!




SUFFRAGE

One of the topics that will be discussed during the convention is woman's suffrage. Women are prohibited from voting and are forced to stay away from politics. This is one of the many examples of the unjust way women are separated from men. If you feel strongly about this issue and/or want to help us advocate for woman's suffrage, please attend the convention!

RELIGION

Another topic that will be discussed is the inferior role of women in the church. Women should not be excluded in the church and expected to remain silent. To resolve this, men should give women the opportunity to speak on moral and religious ideas during assemblies and gatherings. If you agree with equality in the church, show your support at the convention.

EDUCATION

Women are prohibited from participating in education and economy. The idea that women are intellectually inferior to men is degrading and wrong, as women should be able to receive an education and enter the workforce. Women are confined to their domestic spheres, forcing them to live as an obedient housewife for their husbands. If you agree with equality in education and jobs, show your support at the convention.
